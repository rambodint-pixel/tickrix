Our Story

Built by a trader.
From scratch.

No team of designers. No focus groups. No round of funding. Just one trader, one frustration, and a long list of late nights.

"We didn't build Tickrix to disrupt anything. We built it because the thing we wanted didn't exist."

— The founder · Rotterdam, Netherlands

— Chapter 01

A trader's frustration.

The idea for Tickrix didn't come from a brainstorm. It came from a screen — five of them, actually. Stacked across a desk in Rotterdam. Charts on one. Order books on another. News on the third. And somewhere in the middle, the only number that really mattered: the price.

The frustration was simple. To check that one number — the EUR/USD pip, the BTC tick, the gold spot — you had to look at a screen built for a thousand other things. Cluttered with widgets. Filled with notifications. Hungry for attention.

There had to be something better. Something quiet. Something that did one thing, beautifully.

— Chapter 02

The search.

So we went looking. We tried the cheap LED tickers from Amazon — flimsy plastic, low resolution, brittle WiFi setups. We tried the boutique ones — better, but still felt like hobby projects. None of them felt serious.

None of them felt like something a real trader would actually keep on their desk for ten years.

That's when the obsession started.

— Chapter 03

From scratch.

What started as "I'll just make one for myself" became nine months of late nights. Sourcing AMOLED displays. Studying CNC aluminum manufacturing. Writing firmware in languages we'd never used before. Building a web dashboard. Losing weekends to soldering irons and Python errors.

Every part of Tickrix exists because we asked one question, over and over: "Is this how a real trader would want it?"

If the answer was no, we threw it away and started again.

"

Tickrix isn't an accessory. It's the smallest, most considered piece of equipment on a trader's desk.

— Chapter 04

Why we stayed quiet.

We're not posting on Twitter. We're not pitching investors. We don't have a TikTok. We're builders, not personalities.

Tickrix should speak for itself. The aluminum. The screen. The way it sits on a desk like it belongs there.

Other companies sell stories about their founders. We'd rather sell a thing that's quietly excellent — and let traders decide for themselves.

— Chapter 05

What's next.

The first batch ships Q3 2026. It's small — limited initial production. We're not trying to be the biggest. We're trying to be the one a serious trader picks once and keeps.

If you're reading this, you're already part of it. Whether you join the waitlist or just bookmark the site, thank you for caring about how things are made.

It matters more than you know.

— What we believe

Three principles.
That's it.

— 01

Build it like it's yours.

Every Tickrix is built like we'd want one ourselves. No corner cutting. No "good enough." If it doesn't pass our own desk test, it doesn't ship.

— 02

Quiet is better.

A great instrument doesn't beg for attention. It does its job, beautifully, and lets you do yours. Tickrix is designed to be glanced at — not stared at.

— 03

Stay small.

We're not trying to scale to a million units. We're trying to make the best one. The first batch is limited on purpose. The second won't be much bigger.
